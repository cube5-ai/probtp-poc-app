# **Mutuelle Comparison Report: ProBTP vs. AXA**

**Prepared for:** Sales Team & Prospective Customers  
**Analysis Date:** October 2023  
**Policies Compared:** **ProBTP Gamme Nationale (Level S4/P4)** vs. **AXA Santé Entreprise (Option 1)**

**Note on Comparison:** This report compares two high-tier, comprehensive insurance levels to provide a meaningful analysis for customers seeking robust coverage. ProBTP S4/P4 represents a strong option from their "Gamme Nationale," while AXA Option 1 is the enhanced level from the provided corporate contract.

**Key Terminology:**
*   **BR (Base de Remboursement):** The standard tariff set by the French Sécurité Sociale for a specific medical act. Reimbursements are often calculated as a percentage of this base.
*   **OPTAM / DPTAM:** An agreement signed by "Secteur 2" specialists to control their fee overages (`dépassements d'honoraires`). Insurers provide significantly better reimbursements for doctors who adhere to this agreement.
*   **Partner Network:** Both insurers have a network of partner healthcare professionals (opticians, dentists, etc.) offering preferential rates and services. ProBTP's network is **Sévéane**; AXA's is **Itélis**.

## 1. Executive Summary

This report provides a detailed analysis of the ProBTP (Level S4/P4) and AXA (Option 1) health insurance contracts. While both offer excellent coverage, they cater to different priorities and risk profiles. **ProBTP excels in providing exceptional coverage for routine and specialist care, particularly for customers who use OPTAM-adherent doctors**, where it often reimburses the full actual cost (`frais réels`). This makes it a powerful choice for individuals and families with ongoing, predictable healthcare needs.

**AXA, in contrast, stands out for its superior protection against high-cost, low-frequency events and its comprehensive wellness benefits.** Its dental implant and refractive surgery coverage are significantly more generous, offering greater financial security for major procedures. Furthermore, AXA's modern prevention package, including substantial vaccine coverage and benefits for adapted physical activity, appeals to a health-conscious demographic. The choice between the two often comes down to a customer's philosophy: predictable, comprehensive coverage for regular care (ProBTP) versus robust financial protection for major health events and a focus on wellness (AXA).

### Top 3 Differentiators:

1.  **Specialist & Hospital Fees:** ProBTP offers **"Frais Réels" (full reimbursement) for OPTAM doctors**, a nearly unbeatable benefit. AXA provides a very high but capped reimbursement (480% BR), which is excellent but less absolute.
2.  **High-Cost Procedures:** AXA demonstrates clear superiority in covering expensive, non-recurring procedures. It offers **€1000/eye for refractive surgery** (vs. ProBTP's €400) and up to **€1000 per dental implant** (vs. ProBTP's €400-€800).
3.  **Prevention & Wellness:** AXA's prevention package is far more extensive and valuable, with benefits for vaccines (~€232/year), adapted physical activity (€200/year), and smoking cessation (€50/year), which are largely absent or lower in the ProBTP plan.

## 4. Overall Recommendation

### Strengths & Weaknesses Summary

**ProBTP (S4/P4)**
*   **Strengths:**
    *   **Unbeatable coverage for OPTAM doctors** (Frais Réels for consultations & hospitalization).
    *   Excellent protection for routine and specialist care, minimizing out-of-pocket costs for frequent users.
    *   Stronger benefit for adult orthodontics.
    *   Simple, powerful benefits that are easy to understand ("we pay the actual cost").
*   **Weaknesses:**
    *   Significantly weaker coverage for high-cost items like dental implants and refractive surgery.
    *   Underdeveloped prevention and wellness package.
    *   Optical benefits are good but fall short of AXA's in-network offer.

**AXA (Option 1)**
*   **Strengths:**
    *   **Superior financial protection for major expenses** (dental implants, refractive surgery).
    *   Outstanding optical benefits with potential for €0 out-of-pocket via the Itélis network.
    *   Comprehensive and valuable prevention & wellness package.
    *   Higher "comfort" benefits in hospital (private room, accompanying bed).
*   **Weaknesses:**
    *   Consultation coverage is good but can leave significant out-of-pocket costs with expensive specialists.
    *   "Frais Réels" is not offered, meaning there is always a theoretical cap on reimbursement.
    *   Weaker benefit for adult orthodontics.

### Customer Profile Recommendations

*   **Young Professionals (20-35):** **AXA**. Lower likelihood of needing frequent specialist care. The strong optical, dental implant, and refractive surgery benefits provide excellent protection for future needs. The wellness perks (vaccines, gym) are a valuable bonus.
*   **Families with Children:** **Toss-up, leaning ProBTP**. ProBTP's "Frais Réels" for pediatricians and specialists is a massive financial advantage for families who see doctors often. However, AXA's stronger orthodontics (for kids) and vaccine coverage are compelling. The decision depends on the family's specific health patterns.
*   **Middle-Aged (40-60):** **Depends on risk profile**. For those anticipating major dental work (implants) or vision correction, **AXA** is the clear winner. For those managing chronic conditions requiring frequent specialist visits, **ProBTP** will save them more money annually.
*   **Retirees (60+):** **ProBTP**. Retirees often have higher usage of the healthcare system, with more frequent specialist visits. ProBTP's "Frais Réels" for OPTAM doctors and strong hearing aid coverage directly address the most common and costly needs of this demographic, providing superior long-term value and peace of mind.

### Final Decision Factors

A salesperson should guide the customer to ask themselves:

1.  **"Do my primary doctors and specialists adhere to OPTAM/DPTAM?"** If yes, ProBTP offers unparalleled value for routine care.
2.  **"Am I more worried about frequent, smaller costs or a single, catastrophic expense?"** For frequent costs, choose ProBTP. For catastrophic expenses (implants, major surgery), choose AXA.
3.  **"How important are glasses, contact lenses, or potential laser eye surgery to me?"** If very important, AXA's benefits are significantly better.
4.  **"Do I value and use wellness benefits like alternative medicine, travel vaccines, or specialized gym programs?"** If yes, AXA's package provides more tangible financial returns.
